Ryan's Sports Podcast — Sunday, April 26, 2026
============================================================

The Phillies collapsed once again, but at least Bryce Harper is still swinging a hot bat to end one of the most miserable losing streaks of the century. 

Saturday was supposed to be the day Zack Wheeler made his long-awaited season debut, his first start since undergoing thoracic outlet surgery back in September. Instead, 

the Phillies watched their ten-game losing streak finally come to an end with an 8-5 victory over the Braves in extra innings

. But that was Friday night. 

Last night, the nightmare continued as the Braves swept the series with a 4-2 victory over Philadelphia, completing a devastating three-game sweep

.



Kyle Schwarber provided all of Philadelphia's offense with a first-inning two-run homer, his seventh of the season, breaking a brutal slump where he'd gone just 2-for-16 with eight strikeouts over his previous four games

. 

Andrew Painter took the loss, allowing three runs on five hits through four-plus innings

. The rookie right-hander has shown flashes but continues to struggle with consistency early in his MLB career.

The series loss caps 

what the Phillies are calling their worst homestand in 17 years

, and it's hard to argue against that assessment. The club now sits at 8-18 to start the season, and 

their .224 team batting average ranks 29th in MLB while they've scored just 100 runs, good for 28th in the league at 3.7 per game

. 

The schedule doesn't get any easier. 

The Phillies finish their road trip today with a 1:35 PM start at Truist Park before returning home to face the Giants

. What was supposed to be Wheeler's triumphant return has been delayed again, and frankly, even when he does return, it's unclear if one arm can fix what's ailing this offense.

Alright, let's turn to the Eagles, who had a busy weekend completing their 2026 NFL Draft haul. 

Howie Roseman made his signature move on Thursday night, trading up with division rival Dallas to select USC wide receiver Makai Lemon 20th overall

. 

The move comes as the Eagles are operating under the assumption that A.J. Brown will not be on their roster next season

, with 

Adam Schefter reporting that Brown is likely to become a Patriot

.



On Night 2, the Eagles selected Vanderbilt tight end Eli Stowers with the 54th overall pick and made a significant trade, sending their third-round pick this year and next year's third-rounder to Minnesota for Pro Bowl edge rusher Jonathan Greenard and a seventh-round pick

. 

The Eagles also agreed to a reported four-year, 100 million dollar contract with Greenard after consummating the deal

.



The draft concluded Saturday with the Eagles selecting North Dakota State quarterback Cole Payton in the fifth round at 178th overall

. 

There are mixed reactions about what drafting Payton means for Tanner McKee, but most agree the incoming rookie is very talented

. 

The selection gives Philadelphia depth in the quarterback room behind Jalen Hurts

.



Other notable picks included Georgia interior lineman Micah Morris at 207th overall and Nigerian defensive tackle Uar Bernard at 251st overall

. 

Bernard, listed at 300 pounds, ran a 4.66 40-yard dash at the combine, leading some to call him the "NFL Wemby"

.

The A.J. Brown situation continues to dominate the conversation. 

ESPN's Adam Schefter made sure to share that while fans are patiently waiting for the impending trade, it's still just speculation at this point

. But 

when a receiver of Lemon's caliber fell within their range, the Eagles embraced what seems to be an inevitable future without their star receiver

.

Now over to the Sixers, where the biggest story remains Joel Embiid's status for tonight's crucial Game 4. 

The Sixers host the Celtics at 7 PM tonight in what could be a series-saving game

. 

For the first time in weeks, Philadelphia upgraded Embiid on the injury report before Game 3, listing him as doubtful, though he was still ruled out for Friday night's 108-100 home loss

.



Nick Nurse provided an update on Embiid's progress, saying the big man is "on court right now doing individual work" and "working as hard as he can to get back," though they'll "see how it goes today and tomorrow morning"

. 

As Embiid continues working to get back to a comfortable level, he doesn't seem ready to push through and play just yet, though the team is keeping the door open for a potential playoff debut on Sunday

.



Boston took a 2-1 series lead behind a masterful fourth quarter performance from Jayson Tatum, who scored 11 of his 25 points in the final frame, shooting 4-for-5 and 2-for-2 from three-point range in all 12 minutes of the fourth quarter

. 

Tyrese Maxey led Philadelphia with 31 points on 12-for-31 shooting but missed three crucial triples in the final 30 seconds of the loss

.



The Sixers bounced back from their Game 1 blowout to steal Game 2 in Boston and knot the series at 1-1

, but 

VJ Edgecombe made NBA history in that Game 2 victory, and his play has been a rare bright spot for Philadelphia in this series

. 

Andre Drummond has provided solid production with Embiid out, bouncing back from a lackluster Game 1 with 10 points and 8 rebounds in Game 2

.

With the Flyers, the story is much more positive as they sit on the verge of advancing to the second round. 

Philadelphia leads Pittsburgh 3-0 in their first-round series and can complete the sweep tonight at 8 PM at Xfinity Mobile Arena

. 

Nothing would please Flyers fans more than to sweep their most hated rival out of the playoffs on home ice

.



Dan Vladar will start tonight after participating in the morning skate, despite appearing to sustain an injury to his right arm during Wednesday's 5-2 Game 3 victory

. 

The injury occurred during a goal-mouth scramble when Penguins forward Bryan Rust fell over him in the crease, with Rust's skate getting tangled with Vladar's blocker, but the goaltender remained in the game and made seven saves on the final eight shots

.



Vladar has been exceptional throughout this series with a .946 save percentage, including 27 saves for his first postseason shutout in Game 2

. 

As Nick Seeler put it, "You know how great he's been for us. He's kind of the backbone of our team. He's a competitor. Always good to see your starting goalie feeling good"

.



This building hadn't hosted a playoff game since April 2018, when the Penguins won to close out that series, and the Flyers hadn't won a postseason game at home since April 2016

. 

But as Seeler described Wednesday's atmosphere, "That was the craziest building I've ever played in. The fans were fantastic and they brought it tonight"

.



Rookie Porter Martone has been a revelation, becoming just the fourth teenager in the past 25 years with at least a point in each of his first three career playoff games, joining elite company with Kirby Dach, Patrik Laine, and Sidney Crosby

. 

The 19-year-old also became the 12th teenager in NHL history, and the first for Philadelphia, to score in each of his first two postseason games

.

Around the leagues, Friday night delivered plenty of drama across multiple sports. In the NBA, 

the Atlanta Hawks took a 2-1 series lead over the New York Knicks with a thrilling 109-108 Game 3 victory, powered by CJ McCollum's clutch fadeaway jumper with 12.5 seconds remaining and Jonathan Kuminga's 21 points off the bench

.



The Lakers moved to the brink of advancing by taking a 3-0 lead over Houston in a wild 112-108 overtime victory, behind LeBron James' 29 points, 13 rebounds, and 6 assists

. 

No team in postseason history has ever come back from a 3-0 deficit, with teams going 159-0 in such situations

.



In another thriller, the Minnesota Timberwolves took a 2-1 series lead over the Lakers with a 116-104 victory, as Anthony Edwards delivered 29 points, 8 rebounds, and 8 assists while Jaden McDaniels responded with 30 points after managing just 8 in their Game 2 loss

.

In baseball, 

the Chicago Cubs extended their winning streak to 10 games with a dramatic 6-4 comeback victory over the Dodgers, capped by Dansby Swanson's go-ahead two-run homer in the ninth inning

. 

The streak marks the longest for Chicago since an 11-game run during their 2016 championship season and their longest April streak since 1970

.



In Kansas City, Cole Ragans dominated the Angels with 11 strikeouts in six innings, leading the Royals to a 12-1 rout that snapped Los Angeles' modest momentum

. Meanwhile, 

the Reds continued their surprising start with another clutch ninth-inning comeback, as Spencer Steer singled with two outs and a 2-2 count before Josh Lowe delivered a walk-off homer on the second pitch from closer Kenley Jansen

.

In the NHL, 

Lane Hutson delivered the game-winner for Montreal, scoring at 2:09 of overtime to give the Canadiens a 3-2 victory over Tampa Bay and a 2-1 series lead

. 

Hutson admitted it might have been his first slap shot all year, saying "I saw some space, lots of bodies, and just tried to shoot it as hard as I could"

.



In Anaheim, the Ducks exploded for four third-period goals to defeat Edmonton 7-4 in Game 3, with 20-year-old Beckett Sennecke and 21-year-old Leo Carlsson scoring just 42 seconds apart to spark the rally

. 

It was the Ducks' first home playoff game in eight years, and they made it count

.

Looking at this Sunday in Philadelphia sports, it's a day that could define multiple seasons. The Sixers need to find a way to tie their series with Boston, even if Embiid remains sidelined. The Flyers have the opportunity to complete a statement sweep of their biggest rival. And the Phillies need to find any semblance of offensive life to avoid falling deeper into what's shaping up to be a historically bad start to the season.

The throughline across all our teams is resilience under pressure. The Sixers have shown they can compete with Boston when healthy and engaged. The Flyers have embraced the playoff spotlight with a veteran core finally getting its moment. And the Phillies, well, they'll need to discover that resilience soon if they want to salvage what's left of the 2026 campaign.
