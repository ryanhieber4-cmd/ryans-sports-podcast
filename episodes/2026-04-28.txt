That is the diagnosis. The Flyers are getting out-gritted in their own zone, the power play has gone quiet, and the goaltending has not had to bail them out — but did not on Monday, either.

The Flyers also have a propensity to get stuck in their own zone at times. Failed clears, lost board battles and errant passes lead to long shifts and eventually, goals.

Game Six is Wednesday at seven-thirty at Xfinity Mobile Arena, and you can already feel the shape of it. Pittsburgh believes. Crosby and Malkin and Letang have done this sort of thing before. The Flyers are home, the building is going to be loud and nervous in roughly equal measure, and Vladar has a chance to put a punctuation mark on a series the Flyers should have already finished.

History has a side here, by the way, but it is a side that should comfort no one wearing orange. Only five teams have come back from three-nothing down to win a Stanley Cup playoff series. The Penguins would be the sixth. They have the players, the experience, and now the momentum. The Flyers have home ice and one more chance to remind everyone what they did in the first three games. Make the next forty-eight hours count. Bring it home Wednesday. The path forward got harder, but as Travis Sanheim said before all this — they'll take it and move on. That's still the right answer.
